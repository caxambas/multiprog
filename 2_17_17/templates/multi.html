This website computes and gives prognosis data comparing the Palliative Prognostic Score (PaP along with the PaP-Delirium), Palliative Prognostic Index (PPI), Performance Status-Based Palliative Prognostic Index (PS-PPI), Palliative Performance Scale (PPS), Karnofsky Performance Status (KPS), and ECOG status which have been validated in patients with advanced cancer.

Please enter as many variables as possible, and this prognostic calculator will provide survival data based on published studies. Please keep in mind these outputs represent survival estimates only â€“ it is important to use clinical judgement to interpret the results accordingly.

Notes
1: In the PPI, oral intake is considered normal if a patient is on total parenteral nutrition (TPN) for a bowel obstruction.
2: In the PPI dyspnea was positive if it was at present at rest. For the PaP & D-PaP, it was positive if it was present at the time of scoring, which for this calculator is assumed to be at rest as well.
3: In the PPI delirium is counted as absent if it is due to a single medication. The D-PaP study makes no such allowance for medication induced delirium, hence there are three options.
4: This is the clinical symptom of anorexia (ie, lack of appetite), not to be confused with cachexia or weight loss.
