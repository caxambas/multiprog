Estimating Prognosis in Advanced Cancer Patients Using Multiple Prognostic Models

This website gives prognostic data based on the Palliative Prognostic Score (PaP along with the Delirium-PaP), Palliative Prognostic Index (PPI), Performance Status-Based Palliative Prognostic Index (PS-PPI), Palliative Performance Scale (PPS) and various Performance Status Scales (KPS, PPS, and ECOG) in patients with advanced cancer.

Enter as many variables as possible and this prognostic calculator will provide survival data based on published studies.

Notes:
  1. In the PPI, oral intake is considered normal if a patient is on total parenteral nutrition (TPN) for a bowel obstruction.
  2. In the PPI dyspnea was positive if it was at present at rest. For the PaP & D-PaP, it was positive if it was present at the time of scoring, which for this calculator is assumed to be at rest as well.
  3. In the PPI delirium is counted as absent if it is due to a single medication. The D-PaP study makes no such allowance for medication induced delirium, hence there are three options.
  4. This is the clinical symptom of anorexia (ie, lack of appetite), not to be confused with cachxia or weight loss.
  5. In the PaP and D-PaP models, scoring for for low WBCs or high lymphocytes was not reported. In practice we input low WBCs or high lymphocyte counts as "normal".
